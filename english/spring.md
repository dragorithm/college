# Spring Festival

## I. Cosmic Background: The Winter–Spring Cycle

### Winter

1. Life sleeps
1. The earth rests

### Spring

1. Yang energy returns
1. All things revive

## II. Core Symbol: The Loong

### Origin of the Loong

1. Cloud forms
1. Wind and rain
1. Flowing water vapour

### Meaning of the Loong

1. Spring energy
1. Rain
1. Life force

### Essence

1. Loong = the embodiment of spring, among many other meanings

## III. Ritual Structure of the Spring Festival

### Twelfth Lunar Month

1. House cleaning
1. Food preparation
1. Ancestor worship
1. Preparing to welcome the new

### New Year's Eve

1. Staying awake through the night
1. Family reunion dinner
1. Waiting for the arrival of spring energy

### First Lunar Month

1. New Year greetings
1. Sharing food
1. Sharing the vitality of life

### Lantern Festival

1. Lantern lights
1. Loong lantern dance
1. The circulation of spring energy

## IV. Completion of the Cosmic Cycle

### Inviting the Loong

1. Inviting the coming of spring energy

### Spring arrives on earth

1. All things come back to life

### Sending off the Loong

1. Spring energy returns to its place
1. Awaiting the next yearly cycle

## Primordial Waters

In many ancient cosmologies, life begins in primordial waters.

The loong, as a being of clouds, rain, and flowing vapour,
belongs to this realm of living waters.

## The Four Great Civilisations

Primordial waters appear in many early cultures:

- Egyptian — Nun  
- Mesopotamian — Apsu & Tiamat  
- Indian — Sarasvati  
- Early Chinese cosmology — Undifferentiated Waters
